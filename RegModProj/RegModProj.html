Using the mtcars dataset, we will answer whether automatic or manual transmissions are better for MPG and quantify the difference, if any, between the two transmission types.

In view of the limiting page requirements for this report, its assumed the reader knows how to load the mtcars dataset and that the am column refers to type of transmission (0=automatic, 1=manual). The code for some of the more basic assumptions not having to do with the analyses wont be shown.

Exploratory Data Analysis

##               mpg cyl disp  hp drat    wt  qsec vs am gear carb
## Mazda RX4      21   6  160 110  3.9 2.620 16.46  0  1    4    4
## Mazda RX4 Wag  21   6  160 110  3.9 2.875 17.02  0  1    4    4
attach(mtcars)
par(mfrow=c(2,2), omi=c(0,0,0,0), mai=c(.7,.7,.2,.2))
plot(am, mpg, axes="FALSE", xlab="", ylab="", xaxt="n")
axis(side=1, at=c(0,1), labels=c("Automatic", "Manual"))
axis(side=2, at=seq(0,40, by=5 ))
title(xlab="Transmission Type", ylab="MPG", main="Transmission Type vs MPG", )
fit1 <- lm(mpg~am)
abline(fit1, col="purple")
box()

We see the data points for Automatic (0) and Manual (1) transmissions. The mean for Automatic is 17.15 mpg and the mean for Manual is 24.39 mpg. It seems manual transmissions are more efficient (average higher miles per gallon) than their automatic counterparts. But lets dig a little deeper and see if there are confounding factors.

par(mfrow=c(2,2), omi=c(0,0,0,0), mai=c(.7,.7,.2,.2))

axis(side=1, at=c(0,1)+1, labels=c("Automatic", "Manual"))
